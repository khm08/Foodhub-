Project Business Statistics: E-news Express¶

By: Kareem Makki

Business Context¶

The advent of e-news, or electronic news, portals has offered us a great opportunity to quickly get updates on the day-to-day events occurring globally. The information on these portals is retrieved electronically from online databases, processed using a variety of software, and then transmitted to the users. There are multiple advantages of transmitting new electronically, like faster access to the content and the ability to utilize different technologies such as audio, graphics, video, and other interactive elements that are either not being used or arent common yet in traditional newspapers.

E-news Express, an online news portal, aims to expand its business by acquiring new subscribers. With every visitor to the website taking certain actions based on their interest, the company plans to analyze these actions to understand user interests and determine how to drive better engagement. The executives at E-news Express are of the opinion that there has been a decline in new monthly subscribers compared to the past year because the current webpage is not designed well enough in terms of the outline & recommended content to keep customers engaged long enough to make a decision to subscribe.

Objective¶

The design team of the company has researched and created a new landing page that has a new outline & more relevant content shown compared to the old page. In order to test the effectiveness of the new landing page in gathering new subscribers, the Data Science team conducted an experiment by randomly selecting 100 users and dividing them equally into two groups. The existing landing page was served to the first group (control group) and the new landing page to the second group (treatment group). Data regarding the interaction of users in both groups with the two versions of the landing page was collected. Being a data scientist in E-news Express, you have been asked to explore the data and perform a statistical analysis (at a significance level of 5%) to determine the effectiveness of the new landing page in gathering new subscribers for the news portal by answering the following questions:

Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing landing page?

Is the conversion rate (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted) for the new page greater than the conversion rate for the old page?

Does the converted status depend on the preferred language? [Hint: Create a contingency table using the pandas.crosstab() function]

Is the time spent on the new page the same for the different language users?

Data Dictionary¶

  1. user_id - Unique user ID of the person visiting the website

  2. group - Whether the user belongs to the first group (control) or the second group (treatment)

  3. landing_page - WhetData Dictionaryher the landing page is new or old

  4. time_spent_on_the_page - Time (in minutes) spent by the user on the landing page

  5. converted - Whether the user gets converted to a subscriber of the news portal or not

  6. language_preferred - Language chosen by the user to view the landing page

1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than the existing landing page?¶

Perform Visual Analysis¶

In [330]:
plt.figure(figsize=(6,5))
sns.boxplot(data=df,x='landing_page',y='time_spent_on_the_page', palette='tab10', showmeans=True)
plt.title("Landing page vs Time spent on page");

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

$ \begin{array}{ll} H_0: & \mu1 = \mu2 \\ H_1: & \mu1 <\mu2 \end{array} $

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

  • The following test to be conducted is a One-tailed test concerning two population means from two independent populations
  • The population standard deviations are unknown
  • Continuous data: Time spent on the page is continuous data.
  • Normally distributed populations
  • Independent populations
  • Random sampling from the population

From the relevant infomration above, sample means from 2 independent populations when standard deviation are unknown and therefore the 2-sample Independent. t-test should be used

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

With reference to the objective section, the significance level is 5%, =0.05

Step 4: Collect and prepare data¶

new page sample std is 1.82
old page sample std is 2.58

  • Unequal population standard deviations - As the sample standard deviations are different, the population standard deviations may be assumed to be different.

  • Therefore: Two Independent Sample T-test for Equality of Means - Unequal Standard Deviation.

Step 5: Calculate the p-value¶

P-value 0.0001392381225166549 is less than level of significance, therefore we reject the null hypothesis.

Step 7: Draw inference¶

  • The p-value (0.00013923) is less than the level of significance, we can reject the null hypothesis.
  • Therefore, we have enough evidence to support the claim that users spend more time on the new landing page than the existing landing page.

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

$H_{0}$: The mean time spent on the new page for each language users are equal¶

$H_{a}$: At least one of the mean time spent on the new page for language users is different¶

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

  • This problem deals with three population means, therefore, one-way ANOVA is an appropriate test if normality and equality of variance assumptions are verified.

  • First, we use Shapiro-Wilk's test for normality and Leven test for equality of variance

Shapiro-Wilk's Test¶

  • null hypothesis- $H_{0}$: The time spent on the new page follows a normal distribution against the alternate hypothesis- $H_{a}$: The time spent on the new page do not follow a normal distribution

Since p-value of the test is much larger than the 5% significance level, we fail to reject the null hypothesis, therefore, the time spent on the new page follows a normal distribution

Levene's Test¶

  • null hypothesis- $H_{0}$: All the population variances are equal against the alternative - $H_{a}$: At least one variance is different from the rest

Conclusion and Business Recommendations¶

Conclusion¶

  • The primary goal for E-news Express,as an online news portal, is to attract more new monthly subscribers. From the executive's perspective, the reason for declining new subscribers is because the webpage is outdated and unattractive to customers.
  • from 100 randomly sampled users, and dividing them equally into two groups: 50 users for the first old group (control group) and 50 users for the second new group (treatment group).
  • After collecting data at a 5% significance level, the conclusion can be made as below for the following questions.
  1. In terms of users spending time on the new landing vs existing landing
  • From the 2-sample ind. t-test above, users on average do spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing page.
  1. The conversion rate for new page vs conversion rate for old page
  • In terms of conversion rate, proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted to a subscriber, (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted), according to the two-sample proportion z-test, the conversion rate for the new page is not equal to the conversion rate for the old page. Therefore, the possibility remains that the conversion rate for the new page is larger than the conversion rate for the old page.
  1. Converted status dependency on preferred language
  • Using chi-square test for independence, the converted status is independent from the preferred language
  1. Time spent on the new page same for the different language users(English, Spanish, French)
  • Using the one-way ANOVA, we cannot conclude that at least one language users' time spent on the new landing page is different from the rest.

Business Recommendations¶

  • In conclusion, several business recommendations can be made as below:
  1. The claim made by E-news Express that there has been a drop in new monthly subscribers over the last year appears to be accurate. As a result, the present website should be converted into a new landing page in order to keep customers engaged and convert them into potential subscribers.
  2. Preferred language is independent of converted status and time spent on the new landing page, the new landing page should focus on the designs (UI/UX) rather than the language.
  3. In order to attract more monthly subscribers, E-news Express should find ways to garner customers at the next level. The company distinguished itself from other traditional newspapers by having faster access to contents and implementation of multimedia technologies relating to audio, graphics, video, etc. In order to attract more monthly subscribers, E-news Express should find ways to garner customers at the next level. The first step should be to create a more up-to-date landing page for customers. If readers choose to subscribe to E-news Express, they will undoubtedly like the material going ahead..
